Human-in-the-Loop Robot Learning: Teaching, Correcting, and Adapting

Date, Location

Introduction

Modern robots, ranging from self-driving cars to assistive and social robots, operate in much closer collaboration with human users than ever. As robots increase their presence in human-centered environments, it is not only important for them to perform useful tasks for people, but also evolve through human interactions. Designing robot agents that can work alongside, learn from, and adapt to humans is a key area of research. However, achieving such agents presents several challenges, including responding to diverse interaction types and human input modalities, adapting to a wide range of users, and ensuring sample efficiency. To explore challenges and future directions of human-interactive robot learning, we bring together an interdisciplinary group of researchers spanning robotics, artificial intelligence, and human-computer interaction.

Paper topics

A non-exhaustive list of relevant topics:

  • Interactive and online learning from human corrections
  • Sample-efficient robot learning through human guidance
  • Human-aware decision-making and policy adaptation in robotics
  • Personalization and user-specific adaptation in robot learning
  • Human trust and interpretability in interactive learning systems
  • Human-in-the-loop learning in assistive and social robotics
  • Multi-agent systems with human-robot interactions
  • Learning from multimodal human feedback
